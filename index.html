This is a log of the depths and the few names
we've given to what lives inside them.

§ 01

Zones.

000 — 200 m
EpipelagicEPI · SUNLIGHT
The lit ocean. Photosynthesis happens here. Most fish you have ever eaten lived above 200 m. The water is warm, restless, and obvious.
FULL LIGHT
200 — 1,000 m
MesopelagicMESO · TWILIGHT
The twilight zone. Light enters but cannot grow anything. Home of the diel vertical migration — the largest daily movement of biomass on Earth, every night, in silence.
DIM
1,000 — 4,000 m
BathypelagicBATHY · MIDNIGHT
Permanent night. Sunlight has not reached this layer for as long as the layer has existed. Most life here makes its own light — bioluminescence is not a curiosity but a default.
NIL
4,000 — 6,000 m
AbyssopelagicABYSS · BOTTOMLESS
The Greek abyssos — without bottom. Average temperature: 2 °C. Pressure: 400 to 600 atmospheres. The seafloor here is the largest single habitat on the planet.
NIL
6,000 — 10,994 m
HadalpelagicHADAL · UNDERWORLD
The trenches. Named after Hades. Found only at the edges of tectonic plates — narrow, deep, isolated. The Challenger Deep at the bottom of the Mariana is the lowest point on the Earth's solid surface.
NIL
We know more about the surface of Mars than we do about the floor of our own oceans.
— PAUL SNELGROVE · CENSUS OF MARINE LIFE
